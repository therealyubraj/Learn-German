1. Präsens (Present Simple / Continuous)

- Description: Used for actions happening now, habitual
  actions, future events with a time marker, and general
  truths.
- English Example: I speak German.
- German Example: Ich spreche Deutsch.

2. Präteritum (Simple Past)

- Description: Primarily used for narrating past events in
  written contexts (e.g., stories, news reports) or for formal
  spoken contexts. Also used for "haben" and "sein" in spoken
  language.
- English Example: He spoke quickly.
- German Example: Er sprach schnell.

3. Perfekt (Present Perfect)

- Description: The most common way to talk about past actions
  in spoken German. Formed with a present tense auxiliary verb
  (haben or sein) and the Past Participle (Partizip II).
- English Example: We have eaten already.
- German Example: Wir haben schon gegessen.

4. Plusquamperfekt (Past Perfect)

- Description: Used to describe an action that was completed
  before another action in the past. Formed with a past tense
  auxiliary verb (hatten or waren) and the Past Participle
  (Partizip II).
- English Example: They had left before I arrived.
- German Example: Sie waren gegangen, bevor ich ankam.

5. Futur I (Simple Future)

- Description: Used for future actions or intentions. Often,
  the Präsens with a time expression is used instead, but Futur
  I expresses more certainty or a stronger intention. Formed
  with werden and the infinitive of the main verb.
- English Example: You will learn a lot.
- German Example: Du wirst viel lernen.

6. Konjunktiv II (Subjunctive II / Conditional)

- Description: Expresses hypothetical situations, wishes,
  polite requests, or indirect speech. Not a tense in the
  strict sense, but a mood. Often formed with würden and the
  infinitive for most verbs.
- English Example: I would help you. (If I could.)
- German Example: Ich würde dir helfen.
